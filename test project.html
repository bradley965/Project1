The Terrors of Ankylosing Spondylitis

What is Ankylosing Spondylitis? Ankylosing Spondylitis is a type of arthritis that over time, can fuse the spine. Often when this happens, you get what is known as a “hunchback”. This type of arthritis is not easy to identify and may take months or even years to identify. I had to see multiple medical providers before they figured out what was going on. I will give you a few things to look out for.

Some symptoms of Ankylosing Spondylitis

      Pain in lower back
      Worse when you lay down to rest or wake up
      Sciatica
      Fatigue
      Back pain that wakes you up
      Joint pain
      Fever

Things to know

Ankylosing spondylitis is hard to diagnose. It will make you think you are going crazy after a while of not getting diagnosed. It is a very tricky thing because it will start by coming in waves. You will have some really good days, or even weeks or months. Then out of nowhere, you will not be able to lift your legs into the tub, bend over, or sneeze without feeling excruciating pain. Something I should add is, unlike normal arthritis that shows more at an older age, AS will show up in your early to mid-20s. So that should be something to keep in mind if you are in pain all the time. You will want to have your doctor test you for the HLA-B27 gene. This gene is only in about %50 of people with AS, but, if you're like me, it will help bring answers to why you are always in pain.

You are not the only one

Ankylosing Spondylitis is a non-curable disease that causes lifelong pain. This can be managed through medications and exercise. However, sometimes, this is not enough.
